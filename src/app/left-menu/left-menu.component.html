Case Study

The situation

You are to assume the role of the recently appointed Regional Director in Chocó Bliss International (CBI), a growing global confectionary company. As part of CBI’s vision to expand its market share over the next 5 years, the Board is launching a new brand of chocolate bars in the country of Palzag in the next 9 months. This range of chocolate bars, which contains 7 different bars with 7 different flavors, should create a big impact with its target consumers, who it is intended will switch to this range from other competing products.

The New range

7 bars are as follows:

  1. The Dark Chocolate
  2. The Dark Chocolate
  3. The Dark Chocolate
  4. The Dark Chocolate
  5. The Dark Chocolate
  6. The Dark Chocolate
  7. The Dark Chocolate

This is a premium chocolate candy brand aimed predominantly at female consumers aged 25 years- 45 years. The brand character is modern, stylish and prestigious. The chocolate candy is longer lasting; it melts slowly in the mouth and has a smooth and creamy flavor that lingers. The cocoa beans are sourced from Mexico, Brazil and Peru.

Your Task

The Board has asked you to provide your recommendations about the launch of the range by answering few critical questions at the end of this case. You recommendations should be as specific as possible and state exactly what course of action is necessary. You could make certain assumptions in order to express your recommendations, however, state those assumptions clearly in your presentation. Try to anticipate all probable consequences of implementing your recommendations and suggest responses to each. 

Below, you will find information relating to the market you are operating in, marketing restrictions, competitor activities, consumer research and other departments within Chocó Bliss International (CBI).

Chocó Bliss International (CBI) and Palzag

CBI is committed to its Vision of producing:

“A portfolio of quality confectionary products, that lead the market in innovation and are valued by consumers around the world”. 

The key objectives for CBI in meeting this vision are:

  • To generate innovative products and marketing campaigns that meets the evolving preferences of its consumers. 
  • To research and develop new leading edge products that has the potential to enhance nutritional value of our products. 
  • To manufacture products in an ethical and responsible way, with consideration for the social and environmental impact of the manufacturing process. 

CBI has grown rapidly in Palzag with the acquisition of existing local brands and its manufacturing facilities. CBI has a modern image and is popular with young adults but is, of course, always looking to increase market share. The target consumer profile for the range is aged 25 years-45 years; this comprises 41% male 59% female and predominantly young professionals. Like many countries Palzag has an aging population with the number of over 65 years increasing. Females aged 18 years to 45 years buy 80% of all chocolate and consume 60%. They are the gatekeepers to the home and buy more expensive chocolate products than males. 

Research suggests that as developing countries become more affluent, consumers switch from sugar products to chocolate. You have begun to see this transition in Palzag, (which is a developing country), and market share should be achieved through natural growth and your superior product offering if your advertising and promotions are implemented effectively. 

 

Based on the data the Board has reviewed, it believes that with the correct marketing and operational plans, this new range of chocolate candies could successfully increase CBI’s market share by 5% over the next 18 months and become the number one confectionary company in Palzag. 

Market Restrictions

Due to increased concerns regarding obesity in children, the following advertising and communications are NOT allowed within Palzag:

  1. Television
  2. Vending Machines

The following communications are allowed when aimed at those over 12 years of age within Palzag:

  • Radio (Programmes with at least 75% of listeners above 12 years)
  • Cinema
  • Outdoor/Billboards/Transport
  • Print/magazines (with at least 75% of readers above 12 years)
  • Point of Sale (POS) advertising and information. 
  • Point of Sale (POS) product display. 
  • Direct Mail
  • Events
  • Consumer promotions and Value Added Offers. 

 All communications must contain nutritional information. 

Competitor Activities

Current research suggests that mass media marketing is not as effective with consumers anymore. The premium brand market is increasing in Palzag therefore CBI wants to increase market share by encouraging consumers to switch from competitor brands to CBI. Some information on competitor activities is outlined below.

Encore Chocolate

Encore has just re-launched its premium brand block chocolate in Palzag with a limited edition pink packet. It has also been training supermarkets and Point of Sale retailers to deliver the core rand message of “Time to Relax” and focusing on the high quality of cocoa beans used. It has not increased market share by as much expected. 

Panca Inc.

Panca has been successful in Point of Sale advertising and information over the last 12 months. It has run Value Added Offers, e.g. buy two bars and receive a free trial bar. It has also invested in training a number of hostesses to visit supermarkets and answer consumer questions. Panca is not expected to run another campaign for its premium brand in the next 12 months and has had some problems with fake product copies in the market. 

 

 
S&S (Included in ‘Others’ in the Competitor Analysis above)

CBI has heard that S&S is working on the creative approach of asking local artists to build sculptures from chocolate wrappers and are sending singers to Key Points of Sale to entertain target consumers. S&S is due to launch their premium brand in about 9 months.

Key Stakeholders

There are several departments within CBI that will support the development, production and launch of new products. Their input into the product industrialization process is described below. 

Product Development- Packaging Innovation

This department is responsible for the development of new packaging forms and functions. It also investigates new printing technologies, new product wrapping opportunities and looks for knowledge transfer from other industries. It liaises with machine, base paper and ink suppliers to produce the final packaging design. It already has a number of packaging prototypes produced and evaluated for Palzag although final sign off is still required. 

Product Development

This department is responsible for the development of new chocolate shapes, tastes and fillings. It is currently investigating considerable effort into creating healthier confectionery options with less salt, sugar and saturated fat. The range of products has already been signed off by the Board. 

 

Brand Marketing

This department is responsible for developing and proposing strategies and corresponding marketing plans for the assigned Brand Portfolio in Palzag. It ensures the implementation of plans once they have been signed off in order to contribute to Sales and therefore market share growth. 

Legal Advisory

Assess the legal issues regarding local country market legislation. It is typically involved at an early stage to identify and advice on any possible barriers in the market, for example restrictions regarding confectionary advertising due to government legislation. It also supports the Packaging Innovation and Marketing departments during the development process to ensure that the packaging and advertising conform to local country legislation. Time needs to be booked with one of the lawyers in Palzag to review all the brand’s marketing collaterals. One week of their time should be booked for the initial review and frequent meetings with them may be necessary throughout the concept development. 

Consumer Research

Conducts market research into the demographics and requirements of consumers to develop a proposed product brief. Conducts market testing of prototype products with target adult consumers to confirm the commercial potential before committing to full production of the product. The 7 bars have already been tested successfully in Palzag, however, you can request further research if required. 

Production

Responsible for all aspects of the industrialization of the manufacturing process once the final chocolate and pack design have been agreed. Manages the implementation of full scale production in the chosen production centre. Production of the 7 chocolate bars has already begun in Palzag and the pack production must begin shortly. 

Supply Chain

Manages the supply chain from start to finish. Arranges the procurement of cocoa beans from farmers along with other ingredients required at the best possible cost. Manages the process of shipping chocolate to distribution and wholesale companies, who then pass on the products to retail stores. 

Sales

Ensures that products are distributed, made available and sold at the right retailers. Develops and implements sales programmes to maximize the availability, visibility and merchandising of brands at the Point of Sale. This means building strong relationships with wholesalers and retailers. Optimizes the sales force, warehousing and fleet management through efficient use of selling and distribution systems and IT solutions. Sales have useful information from the Point of Sale and represent CBI and its brands on the front line; it often feels it is consulted too late in a campaign. 

 

Design Agency

The design agency is external to CBI and creates the visuals for the campaigns in collaboration with Brand Marketing. The campaign visuals can go through many versions as the campaign develops. 

 

Your Stakeholders 

The Board

The Board generally agrees on the fact that new and innovative ways need to be utilized in order to capture the imagination of consumers and to remain competitive in an increasingly aggressive market. 

Julien Bliss is the Managing Director and in line with the heritage of the family business is passionate about innovation as he believes this to be one of the core factors in the continued growth of CBI across the world. He is also keen for all members of the organization to understand the confectionary business from idea conception, through product development to sales and execution. 

Susie Miller is Sales and Marketing Director. Susie is particularly interested in Corporate Social Responsibility and a keen proponent of Marketing in an ethical way whilst highlighting the health and nutrition projects CBI is involved in. 

Christopher Barnett is the Director responsible for Research, Development and Production. He is particularly interested in current projects into addiction and which ingredients consumers crave more, he hopes this new brand could be adapted in the future to include some of his research team’s findings.
